Fall 2023 and Forward Language and Culture Options
Students following a Fall 2023 and forward plan of study will chose from the following options to fulfill the language and
culture requirement.
      Two (2) approved foreign language courses, and one (1) JEDI
      (https://www.purdue.edu/science/Current_Students/all_current_approved_courses.php?
      _ga=2.23317946.1023436512.1676294680-1084721351.1670596101)course from the Culture and Diversity course list
      One (1) approved foreign language course, one (1) JEDI
      (https://www.purdue.edu/science/Current_Students/all_current_approved_courses.php?
      _ga=2.23317946.1023436512.1676294680-1084721351.1670596101)course, and an approved short-term study abroad
      program (minimum eight (8) days containing a minimum 3-credit course and significant immersion in the local culture).
      See your Academic Advisor for more information.
      Three (3) approved culture or diversity courses containing at least one (1) JEDI
      (https://www.purdue.edu/science/Current_Students/all_current_approved_courses.php?
      _ga=2.23317946.1023436512.1676294680-1084721351.1670596101) course. See Requirements
      (https://www.purdue.edu/science/Current_Students/curriculum_and_degree_requirements/Three%20Culture%20Co
      urse%20Option%20.html).
      An approved study abroad experience
      (https://www.purdue.edu/science/Current_Students/curriculum_and_degree_requirements/language-and-culture-
      experiential-requirement.html) and one (1) JEDI
      (https://www.purdue.edu/science/Current_Students/all_current_approved_courses.php?
      _ga=2.23317946.1023436512.1676294680-1084721351.1670596101)course. Once approved, a non-credit waiver applies to
      a student's degree audit, and six (6) credits are available for elective coursework to meet the minimum 120-credit hour
      degree requirement. See your Academic Advisor for more information.
      An approved program must satisfy the following criteria:
             Take place outside the United States and meet one of the following program requirements:
                    Approved semester or year-long Study Abroad program
                    Summer program of at least seven and a half weeks duration
                    Approved short-term study abroad program (minimum eight (8) days containing a minimum 3-credit course
                    and significant immersion in the local culture)
                    Purdue Summer Internship Program
                    College of Education Maymester in Tanzania Study Abroad Program
             Include a minimum three (3) credits of coursework or research
             Include significant immersion in the local culture and language independent of any US-based program in which the
             student may be participating
             Students wishing to use an approved study abroad program to meet the Foreign Language and Culture requirement
             must complete the Experiential Learning Contract process.
            Foreign Language and Culture Requirement Waiver. Students may be approved a full or partial waiver based on their
            catalog term. Pre-Fall 2023 students will receive a full waiver. Fall 2023 and forward students will receive a partial
            waiver and be required to complete a Justice, Equity, Diversity and Inclusion (JEDI) course from the approved course
            list.
                         International Students. International students meet the intent of the Foreign Language and Culture
                         requirement through their international experience at Purdue University and completion of one (1) JEDI
                         (https://www.purdue.edu/science/Current_Students/all_current_approved_courses.php?
                         _ga=2.23317946.1023436512.1676294680-1084721351.1670596101) course.
                         Domestic Students with international experience. Students who have, through family and community,
                         experienced a culture in addition to American culture may be eligible for a waiver of the Foreign Language
                         and Culture requirement. Interested students should consult with advisor. If recommended, students will
                         contact scienceadvising@purdue.edu to schedule an interview. If approved, a non-credit waiver is
                         applied to a student's degree audit (mPP), and six (6) credits become elective coursework to meet the
                         minimum 120-credit hour degree requirement.
Curriculum Notice: courses taken to meet the Foreign Language and Culture requirement may not also be used to meet the
General Education or Great Issues requirements.
